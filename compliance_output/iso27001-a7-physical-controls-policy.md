# ISO 27001 Physical Controls Policy (Annex A.7)

## 1. Purpose
This policy establishes physical and environmental security controls to protect Teamcognito Solutions Pvt Ltd's premises, equipment, and information from unauthorised physical access, damage, theft, and environmental hazards. It is aligned with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A.7 and is essential to ensuring that the physical layer of the organisation's information security posture is robust and consistently enforced.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Teamcognito Solutions Pvt Ltd physical locations — including head office, branch offices, co-location facilities, data centres, and any third-party premises where Teamcognito equipment or information is processed or stored. It applies to all employees, contractors, visitors, and maintenance personnel who access these locations or handle Teamcognito equipment.

## 3. Policy Statement

### 3.1 Physical Security Perimeters (A.7.1)
1. All Teamcognito premises that house information processing facilities shall be protected by clearly defined physical security perimeters (e.g., walls, controlled entry doors, security fencing).
2. Server rooms, data centre areas, and network equipment rooms shall be designated as **Secure Zones** requiring additional access controls beyond the main building perimeter.
3. Physical security perimeter reviews shall be conducted annually and following any significant physical changes to premises.

### 3.2 Physical Entry Controls (A.7.2)
4. Access to all Teamcognito premises shall be controlled by electronic access systems (e.g., proximity card readers, PIN pads, or biometric readers).
5. All employees and contractors shall be issued individual, non-transferable access credentials (e.g., access cards/badges); access credentials shall not be shared or lent.
6. Access rights to physical locations shall be reviewed quarterly and revoked within 4 hours of an employee or contractor departure.
7. Visitors shall be registered, issued a visitor badge, and escorted at all times within Secure Zones. Visitor logs shall be retained for a minimum of 12 months.
8. All physical entry/exit events shall be logged electronically; logs shall be reviewed weekly by the Facilities/Security team and retained for a minimum of 12 months.
9. Tailgating and piggybacking into secure areas is prohibited; personnel are expected to challenge or report any unescorted individual in a Secure Zone.

### 3.3 Securing Offices, Rooms and Facilities (A.7.3)
10. Offices and working areas shall be secured when unoccupied; keys, access cards, and combination codes shall not be shared with unauthorised persons.
11. Meeting rooms used to discuss confidential information shall have door locks and, where possible, opaque or frosted glass.
12. Storage facilities for physical records classified as Confidential or Restricted shall use locked cabinets or rooms with restricted key access.

### 3.4 Physical Security Monitoring (A.7.4)
13. Closed-circuit television (CCTV) systems shall be installed and operational at all entry/exit points and within Secure Zones.
14. CCTV footage shall be retained for a minimum of 90 days and accessible only to authorised Security and Facilities personnel.
15. Physical security monitoring shall operate 24/7; any system failure shall be reported and remediated within 4 hours.
16. Intrusion detection systems (IDS — physical) shall be installed in Secure Zones; alerts shall trigger an immediate response from on-site security personnel or a contracted security provider.

### 3.5 Protection Against Physical and Environmental Threats (A.7.5)
17. All information processing facilities shall be assessed for exposure to physical and environmental threats including fire, flood, earthquake, power outage, and extreme temperature.
18. Fire suppression systems, smoke detectors, and flood sensors shall be installed in data centre and server room areas; these shall be tested bi-annually.
19. Temperature and humidity monitoring shall be operational and alerting in all data centre/server room environments; target ranges shall be documented and enforced.

### 3.6 Working in Secure Areas (A.7.6)
20. Work in Secure Zones (server rooms, data centres) shall be subject to additional restrictions: no photography, no personal electronic devices unless approved, and a "two-person rule" for high-risk operations (e.g., decommissioning equipment).
21. Access to Secure Zones shall be logged with purpose of visit recorded.
22. Lone working in Secure Zones after hours is prohibited unless a safe-working procedure is followed and a check-in/check-out process is active.

### 3.7 Clear Desk and Clear Screen (A.7.7)
23. A Clear Desk Policy is mandatory: at the end of each working day (and during any absence from the desk), all physical documents classified as Internal, Confidential, or Restricted must be stored in locked drawers or cabinets.
24. Computers and workstations shall be locked (screen lock engaged) whenever the user leaves their desk, even briefly. Automatic screen lock shall activate after a maximum of 5 minutes of inactivity.
25. Whiteboards and shared displays used during sensitive discussions must be erased or covered before meeting rooms are vacated.
26. Printers containing sensitive documents must not be left unattended; pull-print or secure-print features shall be enabled on all shared printers.

### 3.8 Equipment Siting and Protection (A.7.8)
27. All information processing equipment (servers, network devices, workstations) shall be sited to minimise exposure to environmental hazards and unauthorised viewing.
28. Server racks in data centres/server rooms shall be locked; keys held only by authorised IT personnel.
29. Monitor screens in public-facing areas shall be oriented to prevent shoulder surfing; privacy screens shall be fitted where required.

### 3.9 Security of Assets Off-Premises (A.7.9)
30. All Teamcognito assets taken off-premises (laptops, mobile devices, hard drives, documents) must be authorised by the asset owner's manager and registered in the asset tracking system.
31. Off-premises assets containing Confidential or Restricted information must be encrypted with full-disk encryption.
32. Lost or stolen off-premises assets must be reported to the Security team within 1 hour of discovery; remote wipe shall be initiated immediately for mobile devices and laptops.

### 3.10 Storage Media Security (A.7.10)
33. All portable storage media (USB drives, external hard drives, SD cards) shall be managed through an approved media register; unauthorised media shall be blocked at endpoint.
34. Portable media containing Confidential or Restricted data shall be encrypted.
35. Media that is no longer required shall be securely disposed of in accordance with the media disposal procedure (see A.7.14).

### 3.11 Supporting Utilities (A.7.11)
36. Critical information processing facilities shall be supported by uninterruptible power supplies (UPS) and backup generators; these shall be tested quarterly.
37. Electrical, telecommunications, and cooling infrastructure shall be regularly inspected and maintained according to manufacturer specifications.
38. Utility failure risks shall be included in the Business Continuity Plan (BCP).

### 3.12 Cabling Security (A.7.12)
39. Power and network cabling shall be protected from interception, interference, and damage; cable runs in Secure Zones shall be documented and physically protected (conduit, cable trays).
40. Network cabling shall be clearly labelled; unlabelled cables in Secure Zones shall be investigated and removed or documented.
41. Unused network ports in public areas and meeting rooms shall be disabled.

### 3.13 Equipment Maintenance (A.7.13)
42. All information processing equipment shall be maintained according to manufacturer-recommended service intervals; maintenance records shall be retained for the asset lifecycle plus 2 years.
43. Only authorised personnel (internal or contracted maintenance providers) shall perform equipment maintenance.
44. Maintenance activities on equipment containing sensitive data shall be supervised by an authorised Teamcognito employee; data shall be securely wiped before equipment is handed to external maintenance personnel where technically feasible.

### 3.14 Secure Disposal or Re-use of Equipment (A.7.14)
45. All equipment containing storage media (hard drives, SSDs, USB ports, mobile devices) shall be subject to secure data sanitisation before disposal, re-use, or return to a vendor.
46. Data sanitisation shall follow NIST SP 800-88 guidelines (Clear, Purge, or Destroy as appropriate for classification level); a Certificate of Destruction shall be obtained for physical destruction.
47. Disposal of equipment shall be performed by approved vendors; certificates of secure disposal shall be retained for a minimum of 5 years.

## 5. Controls and Procedures

### 5.1 Access Control Procedure
- Access card provisioning managed by Facilities; request requires HR confirmation.
- Quarterly access review by Facilities and CISO; terminated users removed same day.
- Visitor log maintained at reception; Secure Zone visits require CISO/IT Manager pre-approval.

### 5.2 Environmental Monitoring
- Data centre/server room temperature, humidity, smoke, and flood sensors connected to a central monitoring platform with 24/7 alerting (SMS, email, ITSM ticket).
- Annual environmental risk assessment conducted by Facilities Manager.

### 5.3 Media Disposal Procedure
- IT team maintains a decommissioned media register.
- Blancco or equivalent certified data erasure tool used for HDD/SSD sanitisation; report retained.
- Physical destruction (shredding/degaussing) used for media that cannot be sanitised.
- Vendor Certificate of Destruction filed in asset management system.

### 5.4 Clear Desk Compliance
- Random clear desk audits conducted monthly by Security team.
- Non-compliance reported to line manager and CISO; repeat offences escalated to HR.

## 6. Exceptions
Exceptions (e.g., temporary desk storage of sensitive documents for a working session) must be approved by the relevant line manager and documented. Exceptions to Secure Zone access controls require CISO approval. All exceptions are time-limited and logged in the exception register.

## 7. Non-Compliance
Violations of physical security controls (e.g., tailgating, sharing access cards, leaving sensitive data unattended) are treated as serious policy breaches. Disciplinary action may include formal warning, suspension, or termination, depending on severity and intent. Theft or deliberate damage to equipment will be referred to law enforcement.
