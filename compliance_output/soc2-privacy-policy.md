## 1. Purpose
This policy establishes Teamcognito Solutions Pvt Ltd's controls for the collection, use, retention, disposal, and disclosure of personal information — in alignment with the SOC 2 Privacy Trust Service Criteria (P1–P8) and applicable privacy regulations including the India Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 (DPDP Act) and the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). Privacy is a fundamental right; this policy ensures that personal data is handled responsibly, transparently, and with respect for data subject rights.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all personal information (personally identifiable information/PII) processed by Teamcognito Solutions Pvt Ltd, including:
- Employee and contractor personal data
- Client contact and account data
- End-user data processed on behalf of clients (as a data processor)
- Prospective employee and client data
- Website visitor data and marketing contact data

It applies to all business functions, systems, and personnel involved in the processing of personal data, and to all personal data regardless of format (digital, physical, verbal).

## 3. Policy Statement

### 3.1 Privacy Notice (P1.1)
1. Teamcognito shall maintain a clear, accurate, and accessible Privacy Notice (published on the company website) that informs data subjects about:
   - What personal data is collected and why (purpose)
   - The legal basis for processing (consent, contract, legitimate interest, legal obligation)
   - How long data is retained
   - Who data is shared with (third parties, sub-processors)
   - Data subject rights and how to exercise them
   - Contact details for the Data Protection Officer (DPO)
2. The Privacy Notice shall be reviewed and updated at minimum annually and immediately following any material change to data processing activities.
3. For employee data, a separate Employee Privacy Notice shall be provided at the point of hire and updated whenever processing activities change.

### 3.2 Consent and Choice (P2.1)
4. Where consent is the legal basis for processing, explicit, freely given, informed, and unambiguous consent shall be obtained from data subjects before processing commences.
5. Consent records shall be maintained documenting: who consented, when, to what, and via which mechanism; consent records shall be retained for the duration of the processing activity plus 3 years.
6. Data subjects shall have the ability to withdraw consent at any time; withdrawal shall be as easy as providing consent; systems shall be capable of processing consent withdrawal without undue delay.
7. Consent shall not be bundled with other terms; data subjects must be able to consent to or refuse specific processing activities independently.

### 3.3 Collection of Personal Information (P3.1 / P3.2)
8. Personal data shall be collected only for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes; collection beyond what is necessary for the stated purpose is prohibited (data minimisation principle).
9. Personal data shall be collected only from reliable, lawful sources; where data is collected from third parties, appropriate legal basis and source documentation shall be maintained.
10. Collection of special categories of personal data (health data, biometric data, racial/ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs) shall require explicit consent or another lawful basis under applicable law, with enhanced security controls.

### 3.4 Use, Retention, and Disposal of Personal Information (P4.1 / P4.2 / P4.3)
11. Personal data shall be used only for the purposes for which it was collected; secondary use shall require a new legal basis and, where required, notification to data subjects.
12. Personal data retention schedules shall be documented in the Data Retention Register and shall comply with applicable legal minimum and maximum retention periods.
13. Default retention periods:
    - Employee records: Duration of employment + 7 years
    - Client contact data: Duration of contract + 3 years (unless legal obligation extends)
    - Marketing data (consent-based): Until consent withdrawn or 2 years from last interaction
    - Website analytics data: 13 months maximum
    - Job applicant data (unsuccessful): 6 months from rejection
14. Personal data shall be securely disposed of upon expiry of its retention period using methods appropriate to its sensitivity (see Confidentiality Policy for disposal procedures).
15. Automated retention management controls shall be implemented where feasible to enforce retention schedules; manual retention reviews shall be conducted quarterly for data categories not covered by automation.

### 3.5 Data Subject Access and Correction (P5.1 / P5.2)
16. Data subjects have the right to access their personal data held by Teamcognito; requests shall be responded to within 30 days (or shorter as required by applicable law).
17. A formal Data Subject Access Request (DSAR) process shall be maintained; requests shall be submitted via the DPO contact email or online portal; requestors shall have their identity verified before data is released.
18. Data subjects have the right to request correction of inaccurate personal data; correction requests shall be acted upon within 30 days.
19. Data subject rights requests shall be logged in the DSAR register; all requests, responses, and any exemptions applied shall be documented.
20. Where a request is declined (e.g., due to a legal exemption), the data subject shall be informed of the reason and their right to complain to the supervisory authority.

### 3.6 Disclosure of Personal Information (P6.1 – P6.7)
21. Personal data shall be disclosed to third parties only where: a legal basis exists, an appropriate contractual framework is in place (e.g., Data Processing Agreement), and only the minimum necessary data is shared.
22. All sub-processors (third parties processing personal data on Teamcognito's behalf) shall be listed in the Privacy Notice; clients shall be notified of new sub-processors with appropriate notice (default: 30 days advance notice).
23. Sub-processor agreements shall include: data processing obligations, security requirements, breach notification (within 24 hours), audit rights, and compliance with applicable privacy laws.
24. Personal data shall not be disclosed to public authorities (e.g., law enforcement, regulators) except where legally required; all such disclosures shall be reviewed by Legal Counsel and documented.
25. Data subjects shall be notified of any breach that is likely to result in high risk to their rights and freedoms, without undue delay and within the timeframes prescribed by applicable law.
26. Cross-border transfers of personal data shall be lawful; where data is transferred outside the EEA, appropriate safeguards (Standard Contractual Clauses, Adequacy Decision, Binding Corporate Rules) shall be in place; for transfers from India, DPDP Act requirements shall be followed.

### 3.7 Data Quality (P7.1)
27. Reasonable steps shall be taken to ensure that personal data is accurate, complete, and up to date; data quality processes shall be implemented for key personal data systems (HR, CRM, client onboarding).
28. Data subjects shall be encouraged to notify Teamcognito of any changes to their personal data; processes shall be in place to receive and action such updates promptly.
29. Annual data quality reviews shall be conducted on key personal data systems; results shall be reported to the DPO.

### 3.8 Privacy Monitoring and Enforcement (P8.1)
30. Compliance with this privacy policy and applicable privacy laws shall be monitored through: internal audits (annual), DPO assessments, DSAR response time monitoring, breach notification tracking, and sub-processor review.
31. Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs) / Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) shall be conducted for all new or significantly changed processing activities that are likely to result in high risk to data subjects' rights and freedoms.
32. The DPO shall report privacy compliance status to the CISO and ISSC quarterly; material privacy risks shall be escalated to the Board.
33. Teamcognito shall maintain a Record of Processing Activities (RoPA) as required by GDPR Article 30 and India's DPDP Act; the RoPA shall be reviewed and updated at minimum annually.
34. Privacy incidents (including data breaches involving personal data) shall be reported to the DPO within 1 hour of discovery; the DPO shall assess notification obligations within 4 hours.

## 5. Controls and Procedures

### 5.1 DSAR Process
- DSAR portal/email monitored daily by DPO.
- Identity verification procedure documented; ID verification completed before data released.
- 30-day response clock tracked in ITSM; DPO receives automated reminders at day 20.
- DSAR register maintained in GRC tool; retained for 5 years.

### 5.2 Sub-Processor Management
- Sub-processor register maintained by DPO; reviewed and updated quarterly.
- New sub-processor onboarding requires DPA review by Legal before engagement.
- Annual sub-processor security review (questionnaire or certification review).

### 5.3 Privacy Training
- Annual privacy module included in security awareness training programme.
- Role-specific GDPR/DPDP Act training for HR, Marketing, Legal, and Data Engineering teams.
- Training completion tracked in LMS; non-completion escalated to line managers.

### 5.4 DPIA Process
- DPIA screening question included in new project/system initiation form.
- DPIAs conducted using a standardised template; reviewed by DPO and Legal; high-risk DPIAs reviewed by CISO.
- DPIA register maintained in GRC tool.

## 6. Exceptions
Processing of personal data outside the stated purposes, retention beyond scheduled periods, or disclosure to unapproved parties requires CISO and DPO approval with documented justification and legal basis. Emergency exceptions (e.g., law enforcement requests) shall be reviewed by Legal Counsel before compliance.

## 7. Non-Compliance
Violations of this privacy policy may constitute breaches of applicable law (DPDP Act, GDPR), exposing the organisation to regulatory fines and reputational damage. Individuals who intentionally process personal data contrary to this policy shall face disciplinary action up to and including termination. The organisation shall cooperate fully with regulatory investigations; employees are required to refer all regulatory enquiries to the DPO immediately.
