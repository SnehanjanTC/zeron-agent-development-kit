## 1. Purpose
This master Information Security Policy establishes the overarching commitment of Teamcognito Solutions Pvt Ltd to protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of all information assets. It provides the top-level governance framework for the Information Security Management System (ISMS) and serves as the foundation document from which all supporting security policies and procedures are derived. This policy demonstrates the organisation's leadership commitment to information security as a core business value.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies universally to:
- All information assets owned, processed, stored, or transmitted by Teamcognito Solutions Pvt Ltd
- All employees, contractors, consultants, interns, and temporary staff
- All third-party service providers with access to Teamcognito systems or data
- All business units, functions, and geographic locations
- All technology platforms: on-premises infrastructure, cloud services (IaaS, PaaS, SaaS), and hybrid environments

## 3. Policy Statement

### 3.1 Management Commitment
1. Senior management is fully committed to implementing, maintaining, and continually improving the ISMS. Information security is a strategic priority and shall receive adequate investment in people, processes, and technology.
2. The Board of Directors and Executive Leadership Team shall receive quarterly information security briefings from the CISO covering the risk posture, key incidents, compliance status, and programme progress.

### 3.2 ISMS Objectives
3. Teamcognito's ISMS shall pursue the following security objectives, reviewed annually:
   - Protect the confidentiality of client and employee data
   - Ensure the availability of critical business systems within defined RTO/RPO targets
   - Maintain the integrity of data and processing across all systems
   - Achieve and maintain ISO/IEC 27001:2022 certification and SOC 2 attestation
   - Comply with all applicable legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations
   - Build and maintain the trust of clients, partners, and stakeholders

### 3.3 Information Security Principles
4. **Confidentiality:** Information shall be disclosed only to those with a legitimate, authorised need to know.
5. **Integrity:** Information and systems shall be protected against unauthorised modification, corruption, or destruction.
6. **Availability:** Systems and data shall be accessible to authorised users when needed, within defined service levels.
7. **Accountability:** All access to information assets shall be traceable to an identified, authorised individual.
8. **Least Privilege:** Access rights shall be the minimum necessary to fulfil legitimate business functions.
9. **Defence in Depth:** Security controls shall be layered so that no single point of failure exposes the organisation to critical risk.
10. **Risk-Based Decision Making:** Security investment and prioritisation shall be driven by a formal, documented risk assessment process.

### 3.4 Compliance Obligations
11. Teamcognito shall identify, document, and comply with all applicable information security-related legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements, including but not limited to:
    - India Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 (DPDP Act)
    - General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) — for EU data subjects
    - CERT-In Directions on Information Security 2022
    - RBI Cybersecurity Framework (if applicable to financial services)
    - ISO/IEC 27001:2022
    - SOC 2 Trust Service Criteria
    - Client contractual security requirements

### 3.5 Governance Structure
12. The CISO shall be responsible for the overall ISMS and shall report to the Chief Executive Officer (CEO) with a dotted line to the Board Risk Committee.
13. An Information Security Steering Committee (ISSC) shall meet quarterly, comprising the CISO, CTO, CFO, Head of Legal, and Head of HR, to review security strategy and risk appetite.
14. Each business unit shall designate a Security Liaison responsible for coordinating security activities within that unit and escalating concerns to the CISO.

### 3.6 Risk Management
15. A formal, documented risk assessment shall be conducted at minimum annually and upon significant changes to the business, technology, or threat landscape.
16. Risk treatment decisions shall be documented; accepted risks shall be formally signed off by the CISO and relevant business owner, and reviewed at minimum every 6 months.
17. The organisation's risk appetite shall be defined by the Board and reviewed annually; all ISMS activities shall operate within this stated risk appetite.

### 3.7 Policy Framework
18. This master policy is supported by a suite of topic-specific policies and standards; all personnel are required to comply with all policies applicable to their role.
19. Policies shall be reviewed at minimum annually and updated within 30 days of any significant change event (regulatory change, major incident, significant technology change).
20. All policies shall be accessible via the organisation's intranet/DMS and communicated to all relevant personnel upon publication and on any update.

### 3.8 Awareness and Training
21. All personnel shall complete mandatory annual information security awareness training.
22. Role-specific security training shall be provided to those in technical, development, and management roles.

### 3.9 Continual Improvement
23. The ISMS shall be subject to continual improvement through: internal audits, management reviews, incident lessons learned, risk assessments, and external assessments.
24. The CISO shall present an annual ISMS Management Review to the ISSC covering performance metrics, audit results, incidents, risk status, and improvement objectives.

## 5. Controls and Procedures
- The ISMS documentation library is maintained in the DMS; the CISO is the document controller.
- The ISMS scope document, Statement of Applicability (SoA), and Risk Register are maintained as living documents.
- Management Review meeting held annually; outputs documented as formal meeting minutes and action log.
- Internal ISMS audit programme planned annually; covers all ISMS domains over a 3-year rolling cycle.
- ISMS KPIs tracked monthly: incidents by severity, training completion rate, vulnerability SLA adherence, policy exceptions open, audit findings open.

## 6. Exceptions
Exceptions to this policy or any supporting policy require written justification, risk assessment, and approval by the CISO. All exceptions are time-limited (maximum 90 days unless formally renewed) and logged in the central exception register. Exceptions posing significant organisational risk require ISSC approval.

## 7. Non-Compliance
Non-compliance with this policy or any supporting policy will be addressed through the organisation's disciplinary process. Severity of response will be proportionate to the nature of the non-compliance. The CISO shall be notified of all information security-related disciplinary proceedings.
