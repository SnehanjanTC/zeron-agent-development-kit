## 1. Purpose
This policy establishes the organisational controls required to manage information security effectively across Teamcognito Solutions Pvt Ltd. It provides a governance framework that aligns with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A.5, covering policies, roles, asset management, access control, supplier relationships, incident management, and compliance obligations. The intent is to embed information security into day-to-day business operations and decision-making.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, temporary staff, and third-party service providers who access, process, or manage Teamcognito Solutions Pvt Ltd information assets, systems, and services — including cloud-hosted infrastructure, on-premises systems, and data processed on behalf of clients. It covers all information regardless of format (digital, physical, verbal) and all business units across all geographical locations.

## 3. Policy Statement

### 3.1 Policies for Information Security (A.5.1)
1. A master Information Security Policy, approved by senior management, shall be maintained and communicated to all personnel and relevant external parties.
2. Supporting policies (access control, cryptography, incident response, etc.) shall be reviewed at minimum annually or following significant organisational or threat landscape changes.
3. All policies shall be version-controlled and stored in the central document management system (DMS).

### 3.2 Roles and Responsibilities (A.5.2)
4. Information security roles and responsibilities shall be clearly defined, documented, and assigned. No security function shall exist without a named owner.
5. A CISO or equivalent role shall be appointed with executive sponsorship and accountability for the ISMS.

### 3.3 Segregation of Duties (A.5.3)
6. Conflicting duties and areas of responsibility shall be segregated to reduce opportunities for unauthorised or unintentional modification or misuse of information assets.
7. No single individual shall have end-to-end control of a critical financial or data processing transaction.

### 3.4 Management Responsibilities (A.5.4)
8. All managers shall actively support the ISMS and ensure their teams comply with information security policies.
9. Management shall provide adequate resources (budget, tooling, personnel) to implement and maintain the ISMS.

### 3.5 Contact with Authorities and Special Interest Groups (A.5.5 / A.5.6)
10. The CISO shall maintain documented contacts with relevant law enforcement agencies (e.g., CERT-In), regulatory bodies (e.g., SEBI, RBI as applicable), and industry groups (e.g., ISAC).
11. Relationships with special interest groups shall be reviewed annually for relevance.

### 3.6 Threat Intelligence (A.5.7)
12. Teamcognito shall subscribe to at least two reputable threat intelligence feeds and integrate findings into the risk management process.
13. Threat intelligence reports shall be reviewed monthly by the Security Operations team and acted upon within defined SLAs (Critical: 24 hours, High: 72 hours).

### 3.7 Information Security in Project Management (A.5.8)
14. All projects involving the processing, storage, or transmission of information assets must include a formal security review as part of project initiation and prior to go-live.
15. Security requirements shall be captured in project charters and tracked as formal deliverables.

### 3.8 Asset Inventory and Acceptable Use (A.5.9 / A.5.10 / A.5.11)
16. An up-to-date inventory of all information assets (hardware, software, data, cloud services) shall be maintained, with an assigned owner for each asset.
17. An Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) shall define permitted and prohibited uses of company information assets and shall be signed by all employees upon onboarding.
18. All assets (physical and logical) shall be returned upon termination of employment or contract.

### 3.9 Information Classification, Labelling, and Transfer (A.5.12 / A.5.13 / A.5.14)
19. All information shall be classified according to a four-tier scheme: **Public**, **Internal**, **Confidential**, and **Restricted**.
20. Information shall be labelled in accordance with its classification, using both physical labels (for printed materials) and metadata/file-naming conventions (for digital assets).
21. Information transfer procedures — including encryption requirements, approved channels, and data-sharing agreements — shall be documented and enforced.

### 3.10 Access Control and Identity Management (A.5.15 / A.5.16 / A.5.17 / A.5.18)
22. Access to information and systems shall be granted on a need-to-know, least-privilege basis and reviewed quarterly.
23. All user identities shall be uniquely identifiable; shared accounts are prohibited except for documented service accounts with formal approval.
24. Authentication credentials (passwords, tokens, keys) shall comply with the Authentication Policy; multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all privileged and remote access.
25. Access rights shall be formally requested, approved, provisioned, reviewed, and revoked through a documented Identity and Access Management (IAM) process.

### 3.11 Supplier and Cloud Security (A.5.19 – A.5.23)
26. All supplier relationships involving access to Teamcognito information assets shall be subject to a formal security risk assessment prior to engagement.
27. Supplier contracts shall include mandatory security clauses covering confidentiality, data handling, incident notification (within 24 hours), audit rights, and compliance with applicable laws.
28. ICT supply chain risks shall be assessed, with particular attention to single-point-of-failure vendors.
29. Supplier security performance shall be monitored at least annually through questionnaires, audits, or review of third-party certifications (e.g., ISO 27001, SOC 2).
30. Use of cloud services shall be governed by a Cloud Security Policy; shadow IT is prohibited. All cloud services must be approved and registered in the asset inventory.

### 3.12 Incident Management (A.5.24 – A.5.28)
31. A formal Information Security Incident Management Plan shall be maintained, tested annually, and communicated to all relevant staff.
32. All suspected or confirmed security events shall be reported to the Security team within 1 hour of discovery via the designated incident reporting channel.
33. Incident response shall follow a documented lifecycle: Identify → Contain → Eradicate → Recover → Post-Incident Review.
34. Forensic evidence shall be collected and preserved in accordance with legally admissible standards; chain of custody shall be maintained.
35. Lessons learned from incidents shall be formally documented and fed back into risk assessments and controls improvement within 30 days of incident closure.

### 3.13 Business Continuity (A.5.29 / A.5.30)
36. Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery Plans shall address information security requirements during disruption events.
37. ICT readiness for business continuity shall be tested at least annually through tabletop exercises or live failover drills.

### 3.14 Legal, Regulatory, and Compliance (A.5.31 – A.5.36)
38. Teamcognito shall maintain a register of all applicable legal, statutory, regulatory, and contractual obligations related to information security and privacy.
39. Intellectual property rights shall be respected; only licensed software may be installed or used on company systems.
40. Records shall be protected from loss, destruction, falsification, and unauthorised access in accordance with retention schedules defined per record type.
41. Privacy and PII protection shall comply with applicable laws (e.g., India's DPDP Act 2023, GDPR where applicable).
42. An independent information security review shall be conducted at least annually (internal audit or external assessment).
43. Documented operating procedures shall exist for all key security processes and shall be kept current.

## 5. Controls and Procedures

### 5.1 Policy Management
- All policies stored in Confluence/SharePoint with version control.
- Policy review calendar maintained by CISO office; reviewed annually or on trigger events.
- Policy exceptions tracked in GRC tool with risk acceptance signed by CISO.

### 5.2 Asset Management
- Asset inventory maintained in CMDB; automated discovery tools run weekly.
- New assets registered within 5 business days of acquisition.
- Quarterly reconciliation between CMDB and physical/cloud inventories.

### 5.3 Access Control Process
- IAM tool (e.g., Okta, Azure AD) enforces RBAC and MFA.
- Access reviews conducted quarterly using automated access certification workflows.
- Privileged Access Management (PAM) tool controls and audits all privileged sessions.

### 5.4 Supplier Management
- Supplier security assessments use a standardised questionnaire (aligned to SIG Lite).
- Critical suppliers assessed annually; non-critical every two years.
- Supplier risk register maintained with risk ratings and remediation tracking.

### 5.5 Incident Management
- Incidents logged in ITSM platform (e.g., Jira Service Management).
- Severity levels: P1 (Critical), P2 (High), P3 (Medium), P4 (Low) with defined response SLAs.
- Post-Incident Review (PIR) completed within 10 business days for P1/P2 incidents.

## 6. Exceptions
Any exception to this policy must be submitted to the CISO in writing via the GRC portal, including a description of the exception, business justification, risk assessment, compensating controls, and proposed duration. Exceptions shall be reviewed and either approved or rejected by the CISO within 10 business days. All approved exceptions shall be logged in the exception register and reviewed at minimum every 90 days.

## 7. Non-Compliance
Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or contract. Serious violations may be referred to law enforcement. The CISO, in consultation with HR and Legal, shall determine the appropriate response based on severity, intent, and impact.
